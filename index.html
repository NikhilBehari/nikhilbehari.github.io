Nikhil Behari

I am a graduate student in the Camera Culture group at the MIT Media Lab, advised by Professor Ramesh Raskar. My research interests include 3D scene representation, reinforcement learning for imaging, and optimization for decision-making under uncertainty, especially for human and planetary health.

I received my Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science and Statistics from Harvard College in 2022. During my undergrad, I interned at the NASA Langley Research Center with Dr. Corey Ippolito, and at the CMU Future Interfaces Group with Prof. Chris Harrison. I also previously co-organized the KDD Workshop on Data-Driven Humanitarian Mapping. More recently, I was a Research Fellow at Harvard SEAS in the Center for Research on Computation and Society, advised by Prof. Milind Tambe.
